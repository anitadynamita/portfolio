Same script, different bets >

One voiceover. Different AI models, scenes and hooks, tested against each other. The data picks the winner, not taste.

This is how I work: every winning concept gets rebuilt as a set of controlled variations, model vs model, scene vs scene, and shipped into A/B tests. Creativity decides the options; performance decides the winner.

How I think >

Concept> Variations> Test> Learn↺ Scale what wins

My process isn't a straight line, it's a loop with data inside. Craft gets a concept to the starting grid; controlled variations turn taste into hypotheses; testing turns hypotheses into knowledge; and what we learn feeds the next round. AI's job in all this is speed: it accelerates exploration so my time goes where it actually matters, the creative decisions.

About >

I believe good creative work is a decision, not an accident. My job is turning a business need into something people actually stop to watch, and knowing why it worked, so it can work again.

That's why AI sits inside my process, not on top of it: it accelerates exploration and iteration so my time goes to the decisions that create value, the concept, the craft, the call on what to test next. Tools change every six months; that way of thinking doesn't.

It took a decade to build: nine years at Publicis moving Samsung, Sephora and Primark, and now at Welltech, where creative systems I design get measured against real media spend. Based in Barcelona, thinking in motion. :)
